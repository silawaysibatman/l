The Shady Side: Cybersecurity Insights

Viruses

A computer virus is a type of malware that attaches to another program (like a document), which can replicate and spread after a person first runs it on their system. For instance, you could receive an email with a malicious attachment, open the file unknowingly, and then the computer virus runs on your computer. Viruses are harmful and can destroy data, slow down system resources, and log keystrokes. Virus vs. malware – what is the difference? The terms “virus” and “malware” are often used interchangeably, but they’re not the same thing. While a computer virus is a type of malware, not all malware are computer viruses.

TYPE IMPACT
Boot Sector Virus Infects the boot sector of storage devices, affecting system startup.
Macro Virus Targets applications like Microsoft Word and Excel.
"Fun Fact: The ILOVEYOU virus, also known as the Love Bug or Loveletter, was a computer worm that infected millions of Windows computers in May 2000 and was created by 24-year-old Onel De Guzman, a Filipino college student, as part of his undergraduate thesis."

Malware

Malware, or malicious software, refers to programs designed to harm devices, systems, or networks. Common types include ransomware, spyware, viruses, worms, adware, Trojans, and rootkits. Malware can slow down your device, display unwanted ads, steal information, or damage files. It spreads through infected websites, downloads, or unsafe apps.

SIGNS OF INFECTION TYPES OF MALWARE
Slow performance Adware: Displays unwanted ads.
Frequent crashes or pop-ups Spyware: Tracks user activity.
Loss of files or altered settings Viruses/Worms: Self-replicating programs.
Increased internet activity Trojans: Disguised threats granting unauthorized access.
Ransomware: Locks files for ransom.
Rootkits: Hidden admin access.
Keyloggers: Captures keystrokes.
Cryptojacking: Uses your device to mine cryptocurrency.
"Did you know? The first malware, the Creeper, was created in the early 1970s as an experimental program."

Spam

Spam refers to unsolicited, often irrelevant or harmful messages sent over the internet, usually via email, to a large number of users. It is typically intended to promote products, steal personal information, or spread malware. Common types of spam include:

# Scam Type Description
1 Phishing Attempts to steal personal information like passwords.
2 Malware Contains harmful attachments or links to infect devices.
3 Prize Scams Promises prizes in exchange for personal details or fees.
4 Unsolicited Ads Promotes products or services without consent.
5 Fake Job Offers Lures recipients with fake employment opportunities.
6 Impersonation Pretends to be someone you know or a legitimate company.
7 Investment Scams Claims high returns on investments to steal money.
"Did you know? Over 50% of global email traffic is spam, making it a persistent challenge for digital communication."

Antiviruses

Antivirus is a kind of software used to prevent, scan, detect and delete viruses from a computer. Once installed, most antivirus software runs automatically in the background to provide real-time protection against virus attacks.

"Stay safe! Regularly updating your antivirus software is critical to protecting your data."
